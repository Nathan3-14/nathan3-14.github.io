Terminology

  • Character Token - What role a player is
    For Example
    • Washerwoman
    • Slayer
    • Imp
  • Demon Bluff - A character that is not in play that the Demon can use to bluff
    The ones in the below Grimoire are
    • Fortunte Teller
    • Monk
    • Washerwoman
  • Reminder Token - Little tokens used to remind you of certain things
    For Example
    • Imp's "Dead" token for who dies
    • Investigator's "Decoy" and "Minion" token for who to show them
    • Poisoner's "Poisoned" token for who is poisoned (ability doesn't work)
    Rememder, Demon Bluffs are not in play so don't need reminders!
    And some of these reminders (like the Washerwoman's and Fortune Teller's) need to be placed before the game starts
    I recommend getting all reminder tokens out before the game starts. The number of leaves at the top of a character token denote how many are needed for that character.

Grimoire Setup

Specific Setup

On the felted (left) side (Section A), each player's character token will be located. Ensure they are arranged in a way that means you can clearly identify which one belongs to whom.
Beneath those (or anywhere you like really), you can keep the demon bluffs.
On the other side (Section B), there is additional felted space to keep spare tokens, reminder tokens etc.

Section C is where I recommend keeping the night Cards. The Cards allow you to indicate information such as "You are", "These are your Minions", "These Characters are not in Play" (used for demon bluffs) etc. to the players.
The death shrouds are located in the same place (but can make a lot of noise so may not be good to use at night).

Night Order Sheet

The night shows what order you should wake people in, as well as what you'll be doing.
The small black circles denote when you need to place a reminder token because of a player action.

Extras

Some things mentioned in the above videos are not directly replicated in my version of Blood on the Clocktower.

Moreover, we do not have a "Townsquare". This is used to publicly indicate who is alive, dead or has their dead vote remaining. Instead of this, I use the small grey tokens in Section D and place them by players who have used their dead votes.

I also highly recommend watching the videos and completing some of the Beginner Quiz Questions I linked above to get to know the general concept of storytelling better before our next session.
